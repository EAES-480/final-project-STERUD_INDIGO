1. Data

Introduction As we continue our societal journey into a new age where technology rules, the ability to extract minerals to produce batteries, microchips, solar panels and more- becomes increasingly prevalent. I chose to do my final project using a public research domain under the United States Geological Survey (USGS), named National Mine Waste Inventory (NMWI). This database has an abundance of csv files, shapefiles, gdb files, and more, and catalogs above-ground mine waste features such as tailings and rock waste piles that are remnants of both historical and current mining activities. The entries and descriptions in the database are derived from publicly available sources compiled by USGS and State geological survey partners, and contain the states of Arizona, Colorado, Kentucky, Michigan, Montana, Nevada, New Mexico, North Carolina, Oklahoma, Utah, and Virginia.

The main fields present in the databases I will be reviewing are:

  • Mat_Amnts: Material Amount: reported Material amount in the waste feature.
  • Mat_Type: type of material reported
  • Mat_Units: units of the Mat_Amnt
  • Calc_Vol: Volume of the mine
  • Calc_V_Ut: Volume of the mine in cubic meters
  • state_abbr: state abbreviated name (AZ, UT, etc.)
  • Ore_Min: Ore Minerals: mineral/s identified in the cited reference/s that is a commodity, or that could be economically beneficial.
  • Gangue_Min: Gangue Minerals: minerals identified in the cited reference(s) that were not economically desirable. In most cases, these minerals do not contain a commodity from the commodity list.

  group_by(state_name, state_abbr) %>%
  summarize(
    mean_state_vol = mean(Calc_Vol, na.rm = TRUE),
    sum_state_vol = sum(Calc_Vol, na.rm = TRUE),
    median_state_vol = median(Calc_Vol, na.rm = TRUE)
  )

3. Possible question and method

Questions

  1. Do certain mineral commodities present more waste than others?
  2. Is there a correlation between the type of tailings and the amount of waste produced at the site?
  3. Is there a correlation between the type of resource and size of mine?

Data analysis plan

The primary outcomes, or (Y) variables in this analysis will be mine waste volume (Calc_Vol) and material amounts (Mat_Amnt). The main explanatory (X) variables will include mineral commodity type (Ore_Min), gangue mineral composition (Gangue_Min), and state location (state_abbr/state_name).

I will begin with summary statistics and visualizations of waste volume across states and mineral types. These plots will help identify patterns and potential differences between groups. I will also consider linear regression models to evaluate whether mineral type and/or state could possibly predict waste volume. If mineral categories are treated as groups, I could use an ANOVA test to determine whether mean waste volumes differ significantly across mineral types.

The results needed to support my hypotheses would include statistically significant differences in waste volume across mineral categories or states, (noted by calculated p-values or r-squared values).
